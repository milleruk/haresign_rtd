# NHS Payments

The NHS Payments tools help users explore published payments to practices and
PCNs.

## When To Use It

Use payment benchmarking when you need to:

- understand published payment totals;
- compare categories of income;
- review payment per registered or weighted patient where available;
- identify questions for finance or contract review.

## What It Shows

- total practice or PCN payments;
- payment line items and grouped categories;
- per-patient comparison measures where available;
- comparison against PCN, sub-ICB, region, England, or similar practices.

## How To Use It

1. Select a practice or PCN.
2. Choose the reporting year.
3. Review total payments.
4. Move to category breakdowns.
5. Use per-patient views to make comparisons more meaningful.

## Interpretation Notes

Payments can differ for many legitimate reasons, including:

- contract type;
- local enhanced services;
- dispensing status;
- premises arrangements;
- population need;
- one-off payments or adjustments;
- PCN or collaborative service delivery models.

Payment data is useful for benchmarking and questions. It does not by itself
explain the reason for a difference.

## Reporting Tip

When quoting payment figures, include the year, organisation, payment category,
and whether the figure is total, per registered patient, or per weighted patient.
